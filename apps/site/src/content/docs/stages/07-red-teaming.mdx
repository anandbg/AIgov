Red-teaming closes the loop between the controls you claim and the failures the system can actually produce. Continuous means it is not a one-off pre-launch exercise.

<MermaidJourney currentStage={7} />

## Adversarial-ML control catalogue

This site cross-lists four open catalogues that should anchor your red-team programme:

- **OWASP LLM Top 10** — application-level attack patterns
- **MITRE ATLAS** — adversarial-ML tactics, techniques and case studies
- **PyRIT** — Microsoft's red-team automation framework
- **Garak** — LLM vulnerability scanner

Match each entry to a regression test in your CI so a known attack pattern never silently resurfaces.

<PersonaSection persona="exec">
Budget for adversarial findings to require remediation work. A red-team that never finds anything is either not adversarial enough or the system has nothing to lose.
</PersonaSection>

<PersonaSection persona="engineer">
Wire OWASP LLM Top 10 entries into your test suite as named regression cases. Each new model version runs the suite before merge. Failures gate the merge.
</PersonaSection>

<PersonaSection persona="compliance">
Article 15 obligations include accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity — red-team findings + remediation are the evidence trail.
</PersonaSection>

## Fictional company

[Sigma Health Berlin](/stories/sigma-health/) runs PyRIT against their clinical-summary model weekly. Findings flow into the same issue tracker as functional bugs, with severity labels that mirror their incident-response severity scheme (Stage 11).

<RegQuote source="eu-ai-act" article="Art-15" snapshot="2026-05-24" regulationName="EU AI Act">
High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way that they achieve an appropriate level of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity, and perform consistently in those respects throughout their lifecycle.
</RegQuote>

**Why this matters:** the adversarial-ML field moves faster than regulation. A red-team programme is the only mechanism that detects new attack classes before they become incidents.
