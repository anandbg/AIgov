A <GlossaryTerm term="Risk tier" /> assigned at launch is a snapshot, not a guarantee. Risk Check is the recurring discipline that catches the system drifting outside its assigned tier — usually because a feature shipped, the dataset shifted, or someone enabled a new downstream consumer.

<MermaidJourney currentStage={3} />

## When risk check fires

- After any change to model, training data, or system prompt that exceeds your declared change-control threshold.
- On a calendar cadence aligned with the tier — typically quarterly for high-risk, annually for limited-risk.
- When a downstream incident report (Stage 11) finds the tier was wrong.

<PersonaSection persona="exec">
Risk check is the cheapest moment in the lifecycle to catch a tier mistake. Once a misclassified system is in production, the legal and operational cost of re-tiering compounds. Budget the recurring time.
</PersonaSection>

<PersonaSection persona="engineer">
Tie the risk check to your release pipeline. A model retrain that crosses your declared drift threshold is the most common trigger. Wire a flag into your training infra that pings the risk owner when drift exceeds the threshold.
</PersonaSection>

<PersonaSection persona="compliance">
EU AI Act Article 9(2) requires the risk-management system to be a continuous iterative process throughout the life cycle. That language reads as "annual" but bites as "any material change" — your auditor will look at the change log first.
</PersonaSection>

## Fictional company

[Acme Robotics US](/stories/acme-robotics/) ran their first quarterly risk check on the warehouse-navigation model six months after launch. The check caught that the model had been silently retrained on data from a new facility that included near-miss-with-human events. The tier shifted from limited to high-risk; the conformity assessment took six weeks; deployment paused in the meantime.

<RegQuote source="eu-ai-act" article="Art-9" snapshot="2026-05-24" regulationName="EU AI Act">
A risk management system shall be established, implemented, documented and maintained in relation to high-risk AI systems. The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process planned and run throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system, requiring regular systematic review and updating.
</RegQuote>

**Why this matters:** every misclassified system you discover after launch costs more to fix than every classification you got right. Make the recurring check non-negotiable.
