Compliance is the stage where the AI policy and the assigned risk tier translate into concrete artefacts an auditor can examine: data-governance records, technical documentation, transparency notices, human-oversight procedures.

<MermaidJourney currentStage={4} />

## What compliance ships

For high-risk EU AI Act systems, the conformity-assessment file is the deliverable. It contains:

- A data-governance record (Art. 10) — lineage, bias evaluation, quality criteria
- Technical documentation (Art. 11 + Annex IV) — design, architecture, validation results
- Transparency information for deployers (Art. 13)
- Human-oversight procedures (Art. 14)

For voluntary frameworks like NIST AI RMF, the equivalent artefact is the AI risk profile organised across Govern / Map / Measure / Manage.

<PersonaSection persona="exec">
Compliance is the line item that scales linearly with the number of high-risk systems you operate. Budget per-system, not per-organisation.
</PersonaSection>

<PersonaSection persona="engineer">
Treat the technical documentation as a living artefact, generated where possible from model cards and pipeline metadata. Hand-written documentation rots within one release cycle.
</PersonaSection>

<PersonaSection persona="compliance">
Article 11 + Annex IV is the canonical structure for high-risk technical documentation. Map your existing model-card schema to its sections before authoring fresh prose.
</PersonaSection>

## Fictional company

[Sigma Health Berlin](/stories/sigma-health/) maintains their conformity assessment as a git-tracked Markdown bundle. Each release tag produces a frozen PDF; the canonical source is reviewed monthly by their notified body.

<RegQuote source="eu-ai-act" article="Art-11" snapshot="2026-05-24" regulationName="EU AI Act">
The technical documentation of a high-risk AI system shall be drawn up before that system is placed on the market or put into service and shall be kept up-to-date.
</RegQuote>

**Why this matters:** drafting compliance evidence after a regulator asks for it is an order of magnitude more expensive than maintaining it as a build artefact.
